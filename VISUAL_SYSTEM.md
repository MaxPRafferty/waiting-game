# Visual System: The Waiting Game

## The High-Level Vibe: "Purposeful Absurdity"

The aesthetic is a marriage of **institutional indifference** and **whimsical irreverence**. It should feel like a government bureau run by slightly bored, immortal toddlers. It is silly, it is whimsical, but it is not "random"—every strange choice should feel like it was made with a very specific, albeit nonsensical, purpose. It should be a thing that began as a concept of something "authentically fun", but became consumed by itself, corrupted, not rediculous at face value but rediculous because of the lack of guardrails placed on its players at its inception.

From a color perspective, the palate of the 70s should serve as a baseline, with rich yet muted colors. These should further be "degraded" when modified or called attention to, with additional states like shadow or highlight mimicking scuffing, rust, shabbiness, and other signs of disrepair and misuse.
From a treatment perspective, the design should make heavy use of animation and visual effects like emoji bursts and toast notifications. The thing should feel alive, breathing, wiggling, flashing. It should aggressively draw attention, to make the mundane nature of it's premise all the more galling.

Thematic examples:

- A house party that exceeds the bounds of the house.
- A 24 hour disco dance-off that nobody is enjoying anymore, but they all continue to win hockey tickets.
- A radio DJ contest for a used car, where the last two people are locked in a war of attrition and refuse to give up in spite of the sweltering heat atop the car they cannot leave the top of.
- A birthday party that someone brought lawn darts and rum-balls to that has gone rather "lord of the files"
- Essentially, the sort of horrible, yet authentic game that no longer exists in the internet age

Language/Copy Reference:

- No "internet speak", like "rage quit". Instead, surface a variety of phrases like "the abyss stopped staring back", a sort of lovecrafitan macho
- draw influence from modern irreverently absurd media like the game "blaseball", the podcast "welcome to nightvale", or the announcers of mXc. Our tone should be lighter, and not be outright nihilistic, but clear that we (the operators of the game-as-characters) no longer care about the wellbeing of our players, and are morbidly curious about how much worse things will get before the end.

## Color Palette: "Vibrant Indifference"

The Void's Comfort (eigengrau, `#16161d`) is the base, but it is now punctuated by colors that feel both cheerful and slightly "wrong."

- **Primary Brand:** `#ffcc00` (Mustard of Authority).
- **Secondary Brand:** `#cc99ff` (Indifferent Lavender).

### Highlight Tints (Applied Randomly)

- **Positive:** `['#00ffcc', '#99ff99', '#ff99cc']` (Mint, Lime, Bubblegum)
- **Negative:** `['#ff5050', '#ff9900', '#ff00ff']` (Cinnabar, Safety Orange, Electric Purple)

## Component Behavior

- **Messages:** When an announcement or status update occurs, a random color from the appropriate (Positive/Negative) pool is applied to the background or text to emphasize the whimsical nature of the event.

## Typography & Scale

- **Font:** Georgia / Serif. The font of old newspapers and legal documents you don't want to read.
- **Base Scale:** 1.4x multiplier on all interactive elements.
- **Checkbox Width:** Increased from 24px to 34px (rounded up from 33.6px for pixel-perfection).

## Motion & Presence

- **Ghosting:** Departures aren't instant; they are a slow, 30-second fading "sigh" of a checkbox returning to the void.
- **Scrolling:** The virtual scroll should feel like sliding a very long, heavy piece of parchment across a dark table.
